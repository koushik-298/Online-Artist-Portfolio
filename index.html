The Story

Where the
Wild things grow.

Elena Rossi is the creative force behind Sienna Muse — a fine art photography and mixed-media studio rooted in the wild hills of Tuscany and the coastal bluffs of Carmel-by-the-Sea. Her practice is a slow meditation: analog cameras, dried botanical fragments, and hand-ground clay pigments layered onto heavy mulberry paper.

Every piece is an invitation to breathe deeper — to linger in the rustle of oak leaves, the weight of morning fog, and the quiet authority of untouched nature. Elena's work resides in private collections from Zurich to São Paulo, and in luxury residential spaces seeking art with genuine earth and soul.

Our Philosophy

Creating Spaces
for Deep Breath

Elena Rossi's creative mission is driven by a deep longing for quietude. In a world dominated by rapid digital noise, the Sienna Muse studio seeks the tactile, the intentional, and the weathered. She emphasizes organic geometries, high-end bohemian luxury materials, and earth-toned photographic emulsions that slow down the viewer's breathing rate.

Each piece is created in small numbered series. Elena builds fine-art photography and rustic multimedia collages using organic elements — dried oak moss, raw beeswax, heavy natural linen, and hand-ground clays, bringing a tactile depth to fine-art landscapes.

She collaborates with residential architects, luxury hospitality teams, and private collectors worldwide to install large-scale pieces that turn spaces into serene sanctuaries.

Behind the Scenes

The Artistic Methodology

01

Analog Sourcing

Capturing natural patterns using antique medium format camera bodies. Sourced natural objects are gathered intentionally along rugged coastlines and stone paths.

02

Mixed Media Collaging

Layering prints onto heavyweight textured raw-edge Japanese mulberry and cotton rag paper, incorporating hand-sifted clay wash pigments and warm local beeswax coatings.

03

Archival Framing

Framing finished pieces behind premium optical museum glass within locally crafted FSC maple wood framing, providing decades of color and structural preservation.
